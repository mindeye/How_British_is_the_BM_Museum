06 — Where were they found?

The museum's acquisition geography

Beyond where objects were made, we looked at where they were physically found or excavated. This is the geography of the museum's collecting history — distinct from, but related to, the production place data.

"46% of all on-display objects have no recorded production place. For every object — with or without a known origin — we asked: where were you found?"

"The UK leads findspot data with 7,351 objects — but this means objects were found in Britain, not necessarily made there. A Roman coin from Suffolk was minted in Rome."

Pre-1500 AD: real excavation sites · Post-1500 AD: often dealer / auction locations

The UK's dominance of findspot data reflects the British Museum's vast collection of domestic archaeological material — Iron Age hoards, Roman-era finds, Anglo-Saxon burials — physically excavated from British soil. These are genuinely British objects. The remaining countries on the list largely reflect the museum's 19th and early 20th-century excavation programmes abroad.

08 — How we did this

Methodology

Source
British Museum's own API
Data pulled from britishmuseum.org/collection — the same system that powers their public search. Every on-display object's "Production place" field was read individually.
Country mapping
1,270 place names resolved
Every unique place string — from "Attica (Greece)" to "Stoke-on-Trent" — was mapped to a modern country. Zero entries left unmapped in the production place dataset.
Known gap
46% have no origin recorded
The museum itself has not filled in the production place field for nearly half of on-display objects. This is reported honestly throughout — never guessed at or estimated.
~7% overlap
Date-bucket boundary effect
The API caps results at 10,000 per query. Splitting retrieval into 11 date buckets means objects with fuzzy date ranges may appear in two buckets. Affects all countries equally.
45,185 vs ~80,000
The museum's own figures differ
The British Museum publicly states ~80,000 objects are on display. Our API search returned 45,185. The gap likely reflects incomplete digital records for some galleries.
Findspot
Always kept separate
Where objects were physically found is not the same as where they were made. The two datasets are never mixed — findspot is supplementary context, clearly labelled throughout.
